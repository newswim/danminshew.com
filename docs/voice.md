# Voice & tone (seed)

Observed from Dan's own writing so far — to be refined against real drafts, not imposed.

## What's already there

- **Long-breath, associative sentences** with parenthetical asides — thoughts that carry their own footnotes. (Joycean in miniature; keep it, edit for rhythm rather than flattening it.)
- **Plainspoken erudition.** Wide vocabulary and reference field, but no jargon-flexing. Says "I'm a dabbler" while listing category theory.
- **Self-aware hedging** ("somewhat," "I don't know that," "I could go on") — reads as honesty, not weakness. Keep the honesty; prune when it stacks.
- **Systems-eye framing.** He notices relations between things ("relative weighting given by proximity"). The writing can lean into this: essays that connect fields rather than staying in one.

## Values to carry in the prose

Curiosity over authority. Humility with a spine. Care for living things — land, people, attention. Skepticism of reductive labels and extractive platforms, expressed without bitterness.

## Taste (standing principle, 2026-07-18)

The site never explains its own references. Allusions stay unglossed — discoverable by a curious
reader, not annotated for them. If a stylistic addition needs an explainer to land, cut the
explainer or cut the addition. Dan is deliberately dictatorial about taste here; the default is
restraint, with flourishes opt-in — when unsure whether something reads as charming or cringe,
show Dan before shipping.

## Anti-patterns (never)

- Growth/hustle/personal-brand tone
- Listicle cadence, engagement hooks, SEO-brained phrasing
- False certainty; punditry
- Corporate minimalism (sterile) — the target is organic minimalism (alive)

## Working method

Dan writes the bulk by hand. Claude's editorial role: consolidate drafts toward this voice, flag drift, suggest structure — never ghostwrite the personality.

# Project brief

## Purpose

danminshew.com is the central node of Dan's identity online — a durable, owned home that:

1. **Establishes legitimacy** — work, projects, and volunteering presented well enough to stand in for a resume or portfolio.
2. **Replaces the feed** — a place to write and publish thoughts, escaping the trappings of social media and reclaiming attention.
3. **Ties the network together** — links out to (and archives pieces of) everywhere else Dan exists: GitHub, bands, recordings, community work.

## Who it's for

- People evaluating Dan professionally (collaborators, employers, clients)
- People who find his writing or music and want more
- Dan himself — a working notebook and archive, not just a brochure

## Principles

- **Minimal, invoking nature.** Few elements, generous whitespace, organic rather than corporate. The design thesis: this is a rewilded patch of the web — grown, not optimized.
- **Content-first.** Prose and recordings are the product; the site frame stays out of the way.
- **Owned and durable.** Plain content files, portable formats, no platform lock-in.
- **Fast and accessible.** Static output, semantic HTML, works without JS, respects a11y.
- **Findable.** SEO fundamentals done right (metadata, schema.org Person, sitemap, RSS) without SEO-brained writing.

## Ties to personal goals

- Write more → low-friction publishing (drop a markdown file, it's live)
- Reclaim attention → this site is the home base; RSS in, RSS out, no feeds to scroll
- Teach / create / travel later → the writing archive becomes the foundation for those

## Success looks like

- Dan publishes regularly without touching build tooling
- Searching "Dan Minshew" surfaces this site first
- The music history (bands, recordings, shows) is preserved here even if MySpace et al. vanish
